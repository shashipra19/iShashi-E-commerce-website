Our Mission

Apple’s mission is “to bring the best user experience to its customers through innovative hardware, software, and services.” And in a manifesto dated 2019, Tim Cook set the vision as “We believe that we are on the face of the earth to make great products, and that’s not changing.”

Inside the multi-billion dollar business platform Still, in 2022, in the second quarter of the year, iPhone sales saved the quarter for Apple. In the middle of a downturn, the iPhone saved the day. And it’s not just about a product. The iPhone is a business platform. Indeed, the iPhone it’s a piece of hardware that sits on top of the software, operating system, and marketplace, which creates the whole Apple ecosystem.

In fact, with all the other segments slowing down, iPhone sales went from $39.5 billion in Q2 of 2021 to $40.6 billion in Q2 of 2022. The other interesting aspect is the only other segment that grew was the services segment. From $17.5 billion in Q2 of 2021, to $19.6 billion in Q2 of 2022.
And guess what that means? The service segment comprises advertising, AppleCare, cloud, digital content, payment, and other services. What drove the services segment this quarter was primarily higher net sales from advertising, cloud services, and AppleCare.
